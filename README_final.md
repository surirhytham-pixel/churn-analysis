## 📌 Project Overview

This project analyzes bank customer churn to identify key factors
influencing customer retention. The focus is not just on analysis, but
on extracting meaningful business insights.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 📂 Dataset

The dataset includes: - Customer demographics (Age, Gender, Country) -
Account details (Balance, Activity status, Number of products) - Target
variable: Churn (Exited)

## 📈 Key Insights

### 👤 Customer Behavior

-   Customers above 40 show higher churn tendency\
-   Female customers churn more across all countries\
-   Inactive customers are the highest-risk segment

### 🌍 Geography Insights

-   Germany shows a higher churn rate compared to other countries

### 📦 Product & Engagement

-   Customers with more products sometimes churn more (unexpected
    insight)

### 💰 Financial Behavior

-   Customers with higher balances tend to churn more\
-   Low or zero balance customers are relatively more stable

### ⏳ Tenure Analysis

-   Churn remains relatively consistent across tenure\
-   Both new and long-term customers leave at similar rates (\~20%)

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 🧠 Key Takeaway

Churn is not always driven by value.\
In many cases, **engagement, experience, and customer expectations**
play a bigger role.
